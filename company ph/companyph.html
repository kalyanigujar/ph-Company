Giving Back

“To live, we need the forest, and the forest needs us.”
– Reginaldo Chayax Huex, Bio-Itzá Association Director, Mayan Priest, and Protector of the Earth

In 2018, Phia Lab launched the Bio-Itzá Reserve – Shield the Source Fund, a donor-advised fund created in partnership with the Bio-Itzá Association in San José, Guatemala, and The Resource Foundation, a nonprofit intermediary based in New York City.

The Bio-Itzá Association, established in 1991, is dedicated to conserving the Bio-Itzá Biosphere Reserve—a 36 km² community forest reserve and Guatemala’s first Cultural and Ethnobotanical Reserve. Nestled beside the El Zotz Biotope and close to the world-renowned Tikal National Park, this protected corridor provides a safe haven for rare plant and animal species that are rapidly disappearing from the Maya Biosphere Buffer Zone.

Through its ongoing commitment, the Association leads the Bio-Itzá Ranger Program, which plays a vital role in protecting and patrolling the reserve, restoring the base camp and medicinal plant gardens, and preserving the Maya Itzá culture. This effort is deeply inspired by the tireless work of Don Reginaldo Chayax and his dedicated team, whose passion ensures that both the natural ecosystem and the cultural heritage of the region continue to thrive. Travelers, nature enthusiasts, and cultural explorers are encouraged to visit the reserve to experience an authentic eco-adventure while supporting conservation efforts.

In alignment with these goals, Phia Lab is collaborating on the development of a sustainable, eco-friendly basecamp within the reserve. Partnering with senior engineering students from the Colorado School of Mines, the team has completed a community-driven design that integrates eco-cottages for visitors and researchers, off-grid renewable energy systems, and innovative water and wastewater management solutions. This project reflects a vision of creating a self-sustaining model for forest conservation while also promoting long-term economic stability for the community.

By supporting the Bio-Itzá Association’s mission, we contribute to the protection of biodiversity, the safeguarding of endangered species, and the preservation of natural energy and wellness resources that sustain us all. Together, we can ensure that the Bio-Itzá Reserve remains a thriving sanctuary for generations to come.
